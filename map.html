NGC 1300

distance from Earth: 61,000,000 ly

diameter: 110,000 ly

shape: spiral barred galaxy

notes: a galaxy about the same size as the Milky Way, in the Eridanus constellation. It's "grand-design" spiral structure is about 3,300 light-years long. Only galaxies with large-scale bars appear to have these grand-design inner disks— a spiral within a spiral.

Tadpole Galaxy

distance from Earth: 420,000,000 ly

diameter: 280,000 ly

shape: disrupted spiral galaxy

notes:in the northern constellation Draco. Its most dramatic feature is a massive trail of stars about 280,000 light-years long; the size of the galaxy has been attributed to a merger with a smaller galaxy that is believed to have occurred about 100 million years ago.[2] The galaxy is filled with bright blue star clusters.

diameter: 100,000 ly

shape: ring galaxy

notes: this galaxy is nearly perfect ring of young hot blue stars circling the nucleus, and is found in the constellation Serpens. Named after Arthur Hoag, who discovered the galaxy in 1950.
